üè´ History & Evolution

1Wickramabahu National College traces its origins back to January 3, 1946, when it was founded as Wallahagoda Central College. At the time, the school operated with only four teachers and 55 students, holding classes in the peaceful environment of the Niyangampaya Raja Maha Viharaya Buddhist temple. This humble beginning marked the start of what would grow into a prominent educational institution in Sri Lanka. In 1951, the school took a significant step forward when the then Prime Minister, D. S. Senanayake, laid the foundation stone for a permanent school building. This construction was completed in 1954, giving the college a dedicated space for academic and administrative activities. Following this development, the school was renamed Gampola Central College to reflect its growing status and central role in regional education. Further changes came in 1960, when the Sri Lankan government implemented nationwide education reforms. As part of this transformation, the institution became a boys-only school and was officially renamed Wickramabahu Central College. Over the years, the school has continued to evolve, eventually gaining national school status and expanding its academic and infrastructural offerings while maintaining a deep connection to its historical roots.

üìö Academic Structure & Facilities

Wickramabahu National College is recognized as a 1AB Super School in Sri Lanka, offering comprehensive education from Grade 6 to Grade 13. The academic system is divided into three key sections to support the developmental stages of students: the Junior Section for Grades 6 to 9 focuses on foundational learning and skill-building; the Ordinary Level (O/L) Section for Grades 10 and 11 prepares students for national examinations and subject specialization; and the Advanced Level (A/L) Section for Grades 12 and 13 provides in-depth education in streams such as Arts, Commerce, Science, and Technology, enabling students to pursue higher education and professional careers. The college is equipped with modern, purpose-built facilities that enhance the learning experience across all levels. These include the Mahindodaya Technical Laboratory, which supports technical and vocational education, and the Information and Communication Technology (ICT) Centre, which allows students to gain essential digital skills. Additionally, Language and Mathematics Labs offer subject-specific support, helping students to master core academic areas with more hands-on and interactive approaches. The Nenasa Distance Learning Centre further enriches learning opportunities by connecting students with virtual lessons and national-level resources. These academic divisions and facilities contribute to a well-rounded education by not only focusing on classroom learning but also promoting creativity, innovation, and independent thinking. With access to advanced infrastructure and a carefully designed academic framework, students at Wickramabahu National College are well-prepared for both academic achievement and personal development, aligning with modern educational standards and national goals.
